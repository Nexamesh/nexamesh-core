# NexaMesh Brand Identity Direction

**Status**: Direction locked — ready for designer execution
**Owner**: Jurie Smit
**Version**: 1.0 — March 2026

---

## System Summary

Master brand with dual product symbols.

The system should feel like **precision infrastructure**: calm, technical, field-proven, and strategically serious.

| Mark | Role | Character |
|------|------|-----------|
| **NexaMesh** | Master brand | Structured monogram · networked authority |
| **Kestrel Mesh** | Product subbrand | Agile · directional · edge deployment |
| **Sentinel Ring** | Product subbrand | Perimeter · persistence · coverage |

One disciplined parent brand. Two clearly differentiated subbrand symbols.

---

## 1. Master Brand — NexaMesh

### Core Character

- Engineered
- Networked
- Restrained
- Modern without looking trendy

### Logo Direction

Custom wordmark with a geometric, slightly tightened construction and a standalone **N monogram**.

### Monogram Logic

The N is built from:

- Two stable vertical anchors
- One angled connective stroke
- Subtle node logic in the joins

That makes it feel like a connected network, a transmission path, a technical system — not a lifestyle brand.

### Wordmark

`NexaMesh` in a custom title-case wordmark with a strong, clean N. Medium weight. Compact and stable silhouette.

---

## 2. Subbrand Direction

### A. Kestrel Mesh

**Feel**: Fast, angular, precise.

**Symbol concept** — a sharp, directional icon from:
- A mesh-like node connection
- A wing / beak-inspired diagonal
- A forward-moving wedge

**Visual cues**: asymmetrical, slightly aggressive but not militaristic, open geometry, light enough to suggest mobility.

**Emotional read**: intercept · scan · respond · deploy

**Shape language**:
- 30° / 60° angles
- Tapered diagonals
- 3 or 4 node points max
- No decorative complexity

**Best metaphor**: a kestrel in dive geometry translated into network structure.

**Sketch in words**:
> A forward-leaning wedge. One long top stroke. One descending diagonal. 3 nodes placed at structural points. Negative space implying wing geometry.

Should feel at home on: a small UAV module, a field case, a sensor housing, an app icon.

---

### B. Sentinel Ring

**Feel**: Stable, watchful, continuous.

**Symbol concept** — a circular/partially circular mark from:
- An open perimeter ring
- One or two sweep arcs
- A central protected void or quiet core

**Visual cues**: rotational balance, orbit/perimeter logic, monitoring without looking like a target reticle.

**Emotional read**: persistent awareness · protected area · distributed watch · 360° oversight

**Shape language**:
- Circular frame
- Interrupted arcs
- Subtle radial tension
- Fewer sharp angles than Kestrel

**Best metaphor**: a perimeter intelligence field, not a weapon sight.

**Sketch in words**:
> A ring broken at one side. One sweeping arc passing around the outer edge. A calm open center. 2 or 3 perimeter nodes.

Should feel at home on: a command dashboard, a perimeter map, a monitoring product tile, ruggedized hardware.

## 5. Typography

### Primary Wordmark

**Manrope** as base for custom refinement.

Why: mature, technical, clean counters, less overused in defense-tech than alternatives.

### UI / Body / Documentation

**Inter**

Why: excellent readability across dashboards, pitch decks, reports, and technical material.

### Behaviour

| Context | Style |
|---------|-------|
| Headlines | Medium to Semibold |
| Labels | Uppercase with slight tracking |
| Body | Regular |

Avoid overly futuristic type.

## 7. Lockup System

### Primary Lockup

**NexaMesh** — corporate, investor, website, documentation, company-level comms.

### Secondary Lockups (hierarchy present)

**NexaMesh / Kestrel Mesh**
**NexaMesh / Sentinel Ring**

Use in formal material where parent/product hierarchy matters.

### Product-First Lockups

**Kestrel Mesh** · **Sentinel Ring**

Use when the product is the focus: hardware labelling, app modules, system diagrams, product sheets.

### Hierarchy Rule

The master brand always feels like the source of trust. The subbrands feel like operational tools within that ecosystem.

## 8. Icon Style

All icons follow:

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Grid | 24 px |
| Stroke | 1.75 px |
| Corner rounding | Restrained |
| Angular family | 30° / 60° |
| Circular geometry | For perimeter-based icons |

**Mood**: Infrastructure intelligence. Not consumer-friendly cute. Not military-heavy.

---

## 9. Surface and Material Behaviour

This brand looks strongest on:

- Matte navy
- Engraved aluminium
- Off-white documentation
- Sand-toned backgrounds
- Subtle copper/amber indicators

### Finishing

- Matte print
- Spot UV in minimal use only
- No glossy gradients
- No glassmorphism
- No neon glows

---

## 10. Brand Voice Translated Visually

**We say**: "We detect, connect, and persist."

**Not**: "We are flashy." / "We are AI because we use purple gradients." / "We are military because we use shields and crosshairs."

The authority comes from: proportion · spacing · geometry · restraint.

## 13. What Success Looks Like

An investor picks up the one-pager. The logo reads as: **technical, credible, regional-but-global, precise**. It does not look like a US defense contractor. It does not look like a DJI clone. It looks like the company that will own airspace security in emerging markets.

Build the parent brand like a precision systems company.
Let Kestrel feel sharp and dynamic.
Let Sentinel feel calm and watchful.
All three marks disciplined enough to belong on hardware, software, investor decks, and international-facing documents.
