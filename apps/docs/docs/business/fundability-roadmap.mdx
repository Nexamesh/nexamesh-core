## 4. Legal Considerations

### Signal Jamming Is Illegal in SA

ICASA regulations prohibit radio frequency jamming. Our safest market entry is **detection-only**. Physical interdiction (net capture) is a legal grey area. This actually helps our funding case — "we're a detection and evidence platform" is easier to fund and regulate than "we're a weapons system."

### ITAR and Export Control

- SA is **not** subject to ITAR — this is a competitive advantage
- SA is a Wassenaar Arrangement member — some export controls apply to dual-use technology
- Detection-only equipment has the fewest export restrictions
- US market requires Delaware C-Corp + potential ITAR registration (future)

### EU Market Entry

- CE marking required for electronic equipment sold in EU
- Detection equipment (cameras, compute, sensors) has straightforward CE path
- No specific counter-UAS certification exists in most EU countries — regulatory gap we can move through quickly
- GDPR compliance required for any data processing (our blockchain evidence chain is privacy-by-design)

## 8. What NOT to Do

1. **Don't pitch as Series A when you're pre-seed.** The biggest fundability risk is the gap between claims and reality. Lead with what's real.
2. **Don't spend money on marketing before you have a product.** Every rand goes to hardware prototype.
3. **Don't pursue US market first.** ITAR, Delaware C-Corp, SBIR — all require infrastructure you don't have. Europe first.
4. **Don't apply for funding that requires things you don't have** (TRL 5+, revenue, incorporated entity) — unless the application process itself is valuable.
5. **Don't ignore the advisors.** Martyn's factory access and defense connections, Eben's financial modeling, Pieter's embedded expertise — all become critical when you're past prototype stage.
