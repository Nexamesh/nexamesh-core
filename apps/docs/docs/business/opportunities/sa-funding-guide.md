## Priority Tier 1 — Non-Dilutive (Apply First)

### TIA Seed Fund

**Who**: Technology Innovation Agency — South African government entity under DSIT.
**Website**: tia.org.za
**Amount**: R500K – R2M
**Dilution**: None. TIA takes a royalty on future revenue, not equity.
**Timeline**: Rolling applications. No hard deadline. Decision 3–6 months.

**Eligibility requirements:**
- South African company (Pty Ltd or CC) — must be registered
- CSD (Central Supplier Database) registration required
- SA-based innovation with commercial potential
- Technology at pre-commercial stage (fits TRL 1–4 well)

**Fit assessment for Nexamesh:**
- Counter-UAS is explicitly in TIA's defense/security priority sectors
- Non-dilutive structure is ideal at this stage
- No prototype required — working concept + credible team is sufficient for Seed Fund
- Blockchain evidence anchoring is genuinely novel — appeals to TIA's "innovation" mandate

**Application steps:**
1. Register on csd.gov.za (prerequisite — do this first)
2. Register the company (at minimum, a CC or Pty Ltd)
3. Complete tia.org.za Seed Fund application form
4. Attach: tech overview, team CVs, market evidence, budget plan

**Contact**: TIA Innovation Hub, Pretoria. Email via website contact form first.

### CSIR DPSS (Defence, Peace, Safety and Security)

**Who**: CSIR Division — government-funded research and development.
**Amount**: R1M – R10M (co-development arrangement)
**Dilution**: None — but IP sharing negotiation required.
**Timeline**: Relationship-driven. 6–12 months to formalise.

**Why this fits:**
- CSIR DPSS has radar, signal processing, and EW competency
- Counter-UAS detection is squarely in their mandate
- They have lab access, testing infrastructure, and SANDF relationships that Nexamesh doesn't
- A co-development arrangement provides resources without giving up equity

**How to approach:**
- This is NOT an online application — it's a relationship play
- Find CSIR DPSS Business Development contact via csir.co.za
- Send a two-page technical summary: what the detector does, what CSIR would gain
- Frame as "complementary capability" — Nexamesh brings edge AI + blockchain, CSIR brings radar + institutional access

**Risk**: IP negotiation can be complex. Retain ownership of the software platform. License the integration.

**Application requirements:**
- Working prototype preferred (have detection demo ready)
- SA-registered company
- Clear market opportunity (counter-UAS market data is strong)

## Priority Tier 3 — Government Procurement / Institutional

### ARMSCOR (Armaments Corporation of South Africa)

**Who**: State entity responsible for SA defense procurement.
**Program**: LEDGER (Local Economic Development and Government Export Readiness)
**Amount**: Contract-based (no fixed ceiling)
**Dilution**: None — procurement, not investment

**Why this is the ultimate target:**
- SANDF currently has NO operational C-UAS system
- Denel (the traditional SA defense supplier) has been unable to deliver on multiple contracts
- The vacuum is real, documented, and creates genuine urgency
- ARMSCOR actively seeks local suppliers to replace imported systems

**Path to ARMSCOR:**
1. Register on csd.gov.za (prerequisite)
2. Register as ARMSCOR supplier (armscor.co.za — supplier registration section)
3. Obtain LEDGER accreditation (local defense supplier designation)
4. Submit capability statement: what detection capability exists, what hardware roadmap looks like
5. Request a meeting with ARMSCOR Technology Development office

**Timeline**: This is a 12–18 month play. Start the paperwork now, build the prototype, demonstrate detection capability, then approach.

**Note on signal jamming**: Jamming is ICASA-regulated and illegal without a license in SA. SANDF can jam (military exemption), but Nexamesh cannot sell jamming capability to civilian customers. Detection-only is the entry point.

### IDC (Industrial Development Corporation)

**Who**: SA state-owned DFI focused on industrial development.
**Website**: idc.co.za
**Program**: Advanced Manufacturing & Defense cluster
**Amount**: R5M – R50M
**Type**: Development loan + possible equity
**Timeline**: 6–12 months application to decision

**Requirements:**
- Working prototype (IDC funds development, not concept)
- SA-registered company
- Business plan with realistic financials
- Some existing revenue or customer letters of intent preferred

**Fit**: IDC is a later-stage play. Target this after hardware prototype exists and ARMSCOR conversation has started.

---

### NEF (National Empowerment Fund)

**Who**: SA government fund focused on black economic empowerment.
**Amount**: R250K – R75M
**Type**: Equity finance, quasi-equity, and debt
**Eligibility**: Black-owned or black-empowered entities

**Fit**: Depends on team shareholding structure. If BEE shareholding is established during incorporation, NEF is a strong parallel track alongside IDC.

## Priority Tier 4 — International Programs (SA Eligible)

### UK DASA (Defence and Security Accelerator)

**Who**: UK MOD accelerator for defense innovation.
**Website**: gov.uk/government/organisations/defence-and-security-accelerator
**Amount**: £50K – £1M per competition phase
**Eligibility**: SA companies CAN apply (DASA is open to international partners)
**Type**: Competitive grant — no equity, no dilution

**How to apply:**
- Monitor DASA open calls at gov.uk/dasa
- Competitions are topic-specific — watch for "counter-UAS", "airspace awareness", "C-UAS sensors"
- Typical timeline: 3–6 month application to decision per phase

**Nexamesh advantage**: Blockchain evidence anchoring for counter-UAS is genuinely novel — it solves an evidentiary problem UK MOD has documented.

## Prerequisites Checklist (In Order)

```
☐ 1. Register on csd.gov.za
       — Required gate for ALL SA government funding (TIA, ARMSCOR, IDC, NEF)
       — Free, online, takes 1–2 weeks for verification

☐ 2. Register legal entity (Pty Ltd recommended)
       — Required for TIA and most programs
       — CIPC registration: ~R175 online at cipc.co.za

☐ 3. Open business bank account
       — Required by TIA and IDC to receive funds

☐ 4. Build detection demo (video)
       — Jetson + YOLOv8 identifying toy quad
       — Required before approaching Grindstone, IDC, ARMSCOR
       — NOT required for TIA Seed Fund (concept stage is fine)

☐ 5. Prepare standard pitch materials
       — 10-slide deck (honest, pre-seed framing)
       — 2-page technical summary
       — Team CVs (Jurie: full; Charl: brief honest bio)
       — Market data (counter-UAS $6.64B → $20.31B)
```

## What NOT to Claim in SA Applications

| Claim | Problem | Replace with |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| TRL 3–5 | Reality is TRL 1–2 | "TRL 1–2, targeting TRL 4 by end of 2026" |
| "$2.15M Year 1 revenue" | No product exists | "Pre-revenue. Target first contract in 2027." |
| "60+ years combined experience" | 2 active people | "15+ years systems engineering (CTO)" |
| "CSIR partnership" | Not current | "Approaching CSIR DPSS for co-development" |
| FAA consultation, airport MOUs | None confirmed | Remove entirely |
| Delaware C-Corp | Not incorporated | "Registering SA Pty Ltd, Q2 2026" |
